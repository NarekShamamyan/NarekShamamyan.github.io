Master's Thesis Project

End-to-end 3-phase co-design of a trustworthy Virtual Peer Agent (VPA) with Armenian middle school students for learning math: from discovery research to effective problem framing, ideation, co-creation, prototyping, into high-fidelity designs and evaluation using design tools such as Figma and Miro.

This project was later published in the IDC 2025 Conference Proceedings and presented at the IDC 2025 Conference in Reykjavík, Iceland.

Design Phase 1: Appearance Design

In this phase, participants were asked to draw a peer agent who they would trust to help them with math. To encourage creativity and originality, no additional instructions were provided.

Design Phase 2: Communication Style Design

In this phase, participants were asked to provide responses to pre-designed scenarios by filling in the speech bubbles of the future VPA. In other words, participants determined how the VPA would communicate in different scenarios (e.g. when they are always correct, or when they make mistakes and self-correct, etc).

Design Phase 3: Prototype Evaluation and Feedback

After phase 2, prototypes of 3 agents were created based on student drawings from Phase 1, which later developed into 6 distinct scenario prototypes on Figma based on student responses from Phase 2. During phase 3, these scenario prototypes were evaluated through semi-structured interviews with 4 students and 1 teacher.
